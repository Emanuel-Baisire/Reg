The purporse of this study is to compare fuel consumption of an automatic and manual transmission car in relation to Miles Per Gallon (MPG). The data source for this excercise is primarily from the 1974 Motor Trend US magazine "mtcars" dataset. A regressiona model is applied to determine a better fuel efficiency transmission between a manual and automatic car. The hypothesis to be tested is whether a manual transmission engine has a better Miles Per Gallon (Mpg) consumption than automatic. The test results indicated that a manual transmission engine has an MPG advantage of 7.2449 using a simple linear regression To ensure that confouding variables do not skew the result, a Mulitvariate Regression was also conducted for a better view of transmission differences in relation to MPGS. Overall, that study concludes that a manual transmission engine fare better than an automatic transmission relation to fuel consumption.
